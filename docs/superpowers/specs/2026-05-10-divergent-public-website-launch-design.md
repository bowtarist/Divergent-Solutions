# Divergent Solutions Public Website Launch Strategy

Date: 2026-05-10

## Purpose

The public website for Divergent Solutions will live at `www.divergentsolutionsllc.com`.

Its launch purpose is trust, credibility, and lead generation. The site should earn enough confidence from homeowners and builders for them to submit a quote request. Each quote request should create a lead in the private app, create a call reminder, and notify the owner or team so the lead can be called back within one business day.

The site is a strategy and marketing layer, not the private operations app. The website should feed leads into the app instead of replacing app workflows.

## Core Positioning

Divergent Solutions is a quality-first regional gutter installation company serving homeowners and builders across Scottsboro, Huntsville, Gadsden, Fort Payne, Winchester, and Trenton.

Primary message:

> Quality-first gutter installation from a licensed and insured team that shows up, communicates clearly, and stands behind the work.

The website should feel focused on gutter installs first, while still showing that the company has broader exterior and construction capability.

## Audiences

The launch site should serve two primary audiences equally:

- Homeowners who need dependable gutter installation and want to know the company will show up, do quality work, and communicate clearly.
- Builders and general contractors who need reliable gutter installation, scheduling, follow-through, and a repeatable process.

The site should avoid making one audience feel secondary. The page copy should consistently make room for both groups while keeping the core offer centered on gutter installs.

## Minimum Launch Sitemap

The launch site should include seven pages:

- `Home`
- `Services`
- `Gutter Installation`
- `Work / Photos`
- `Reviews`
- `About`
- `Request a Quote`

No individual city landing pages are needed for the first launch. The launch site should mention all six service cities across the main pages. City-specific pages can be considered later as an SEO phase after the core site is live.

## Page Purposes

### Home

Purpose: Give visitors the fast trust-and-fit check.

The home page should quickly establish what Divergent Solutions does, where it works, who it serves, and why visitors should trust the company. A visitor should know within seconds that Divergent Solutions installs quality gutters, serves their area, works with homeowners and builders, and has real proof through photos and Google reviews.

The primary CTA is `Request a Quote`.

The home page should answer:

- Do they do what I need?
- Are they local or regional enough for my project?
- Do they look trustworthy?
- Can I request a quote easily?

### Services

Purpose: Show the full capability set without muddying the launch focus.

Gutter installation should be the first and strongest service on the page. Supporting services should be presented after it.

Recommended service order:

- Gutter Installation
- Gutter Guards
- Gutter Repairs
- Fascia & Soffit
- Siding
- New Construction
- Home Renovations

The page should make the message clear: Divergent Solutions is the quality-first gutter install choice, with broader exterior and construction experience when the project needs it.

The primary CTA is `Request a Quote`.

### Gutter Installation

Purpose: Serve as the main sales page for the primary offer.

This page should explain the quality-first installation process, how quotes are handled, what affects pricing, the 2-year labor warranty, and why a real conversation matters before giving a final project price.

The page should avoid generic pricing promises. Instead, it should explain that gutter install pricing depends on the real project: measurements, material choices, home layout, access, downspout needs, and overall scope.

The primary CTA is `Request a Quote`.

### Work / Photos

Purpose: Prove quality visually.

This page should show approved before-and-after project photo sets. Photos should be grouped by project when possible. The strongest sets will show the same area before and after, plus detail shots that prove clean workmanship.

The page should support both homeowners and builders by showing finished exterior quality, project variety, and repeatable workmanship.

The primary CTA is `Request a Quote`.

### Reviews

Purpose: Reinforce quality, professionalism, showing up, and customer satisfaction.

The reviews page should use approved Google review snippets. Review attribution should use first name plus last initial, followed by `Google Review`.

Example attribution:

> - Ashley J., Google Review

The strongest reviews should be reused on the home page and relevant service pages. The reviews page can include all approved review snippets.

The primary CTA is `Request a Quote`.

### About

Purpose: Build trust in the people and process behind the work.

The About page should mention Seth Talley and Brent Wilbanks as the founders of Divergent Solutions. It should state that they bring a combined 30 years of construction industry experience.

The tone should be a mixture of founder-led and company/process focused, with the stronger emphasis on process. The page should communicate quality, customer service, follow-up, and accountability.

Website-ready direction:

> Divergent Solutions was founded by Seth Talley and Brent Wilbanks, bringing a combined 30 years of construction industry experience to every project.
>
> Our emphasis is simple: quality work and customer service. We believe a job should be done right, communicated clearly, and followed through after the work is complete. If an issue ever comes up, we go above and beyond to make it right, and we follow up with our customers to make sure they remain completely satisfied.

The primary CTA is `Request a Quote`.

### Request a Quote

Purpose: Convert trust into a lead for the private app.

The Request a Quote page should set clear expectations: visitors submit project details and photos, then Divergent Solutions calls back within one business day.

The confirmation message should be:

> Thanks. We received your request and will call you back within 1 business day.

The submit button should say `Submit Quote Request`.

## Messaging Strategy

The site should repeatedly reinforce three ideas:

- Divergent Solutions does quality-first gutter installation.
- Divergent Solutions shows up, communicates clearly, and follows through.
- Divergent Solutions has real proof: licensed and insured status, a 2-year labor warranty, Google reviews, before-and-after photos, and 30 years combined construction experience.

The site should not feel like a broad general contractor site that happens to do gutters. It should feel like a regional gutter installation specialist with broader exterior and construction capability.

### Warranty Language

The site should mention the warranty briefly in plain language:

> Our gutter installations include a 2-year labor warranty covering installation workmanship. The warranty does not cover outside damage such as roof leaks, mower damage, tree damage, storm damage, or other issues unrelated to the installation.

Formal warranty details can live later in quote documents or customer paperwork.

## Lead Workflow

The launch lead workflow should be:

`Quote request submitted -> lead created in private app -> call reminder created -> owner/team notified -> lead followed up`

Operationally, this means:

- A website visitor submits a quote request.
- The private app creates a lead with status `New Web Lead`.
- The app stores contact information, address, customer type, project type, notes, and uploaded photos.
- The app creates a task or reminder: `Call new website lead`.
- The owner or team receives an immediate notification.
- After the call, the lead can move to statuses such as `Contacted`, `Visit Scheduled`, `Quoted`, `Won`, or `Lost`.

The website should not promise instant online pricing. It should promise a callback within one business day.

## Customer Objections

The site should answer these objections before the visitor has to ask them.

### How much will it cost?

Answer with clear quote-process language, not generic pricing guesses.

The site should explain that pricing depends on real project details such as measurements, material choices, home layout, access, downspouts, and project scope. The quote process is part of doing the job correctly.

### Will you show up?

Answer with the one-business-day callback promise, process language, and reviews that mention reliability.

The site should emphasize clear communication, scheduled next steps, and dependable follow-through.

### Will the work be good?

Answer with before-and-after photos, Google reviews, licensed and insured status, quality-first messaging, and the 2-year labor warranty.

### Do you serve my area?

Answer by naming the service cities clearly:

- Scottsboro
- Huntsville
- Gadsden
- Fort Payne
- Winchester
- Trenton

### Do you work with my type of customer?

Answer by speaking to homeowners and builders throughout the site. Both should feel like primary audiences.

### Can I trust you around my home or job site?

Answer through founder credibility, licensed and insured status, customer reviews, workmanship standards, cleanup language, and the follow-up commitment.

### Is my quote request going into a black hole?

Answer through the quote page, confirmation message, and lead workflow. The site should clearly say that Divergent Solutions will call back within one business day.

## Success Criteria

The launch site is successful if it:

- Makes the company look credible within the first few seconds.
- Clearly communicates gutter installs as the primary service.
- Speaks to homeowners and builders without making either feel secondary.
- Answers the top concerns around cost and reliability.
- Uses real proof through photos and Google reviews.
- Makes `Request a Quote` easy to find from every major page.
- Sets the callback expectation clearly.
- Feeds quote requests into the private app as actionable leads with call reminders.
