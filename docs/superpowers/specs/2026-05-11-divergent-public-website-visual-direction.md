# Divergent Solutions Public Website Visual Direction

Date: 2026-05-11

## Purpose

This document records the approved visual direction for the Divergent Solutions public website after reviewing browser-based concept boards.

It builds on the approved launch strategy in `2026-05-10-divergent-public-website-launch-design.md`. This document does not define implementation details. It defines the visual target for the next design and build phase.

## Approved Direction

The approved visual direction is:

> Process-first regional trust.

The site should feel warm, dependable, and professional, with a clear emphasis on process, follow-through, and regional service coverage. It should look credible to both homeowners and builders.

The selected direction combines:

- `B2. Process First` as the primary structure and tone
- `B1. Warm Service` for visible service-area coverage
- Scottsboro new construction as the primary homepage hero image
- Woodville and Stevenson Church as supporting proof lower on the page

## First Impression

The homepage should lead with a process-focused message:

> Built around a better install process.

This headline direction supports the company promise: quality work, clear communication, dependable follow-through, and a callback within one business day.

The first screen should quickly communicate:

- Licensed and insured gutter installation
- Quality-first process
- Homeowners and builders both served
- Service area visibility
- Request a Quote CTA
- Real project photography
- Google review proof

## Visual Tone

The site should feel:

- Warm
- Regional
- Process-led
- Trustworthy
- Practical
- Established
- Quality-first

It should avoid feeling:

- Like a generic contractor template
- Too luxury or high-end for the local market
- Too dark or industrial
- Too soft or casual
- Too broad/general-contractor focused

## Palette Direction

The approved color direction is warm green and cream with red CTA accents.

Concept palette:

- Warm cream background: `#f7f5ed`
- Off-white panel/background: `#fffdf7`
- Deep green primary: `#243a30`
- Soft green accent: `#dfe7dc`
- Muted text: `#646b61`
- Red CTA accent: `#a92a2a`
- Gold proof accent: `#c59b46`

The exact final color values can be refined during implementation, but the site should keep this relationship:

- Cream/off-white for warmth and trust
- Deep green for process, dependability, and regional character
- Red only for important action moments like `Request a Quote`
- Gold as a restrained proof/accent color

The site should not become a one-color palette. The green should lead the brand system, but the red CTA and gold proof accents should give the interface contrast and urgency.

## Typography Direction

The visual concept used a high-trust editorial serif for large headlines and a clear sans-serif for navigation, body copy, forms, and interface text.

The final site should preserve this relationship:

- Serif display type for major headlines and trust-building statements
- Clean sans-serif for body copy, navigation, buttons, forms, proof points, and process steps
- No overly decorative or novelty typography
- No cramped or overly stylized type

The type should feel established and readable, not trendy for its own sake.

## Homepage Structure

The approved homepage direction should use this structure:

1. Top navigation with logo, page links, and `Request a Quote`
2. Split hero section with process-led copy on the left and the Scottsboro new construction photo on the right
3. Visible service-area chips in the hero
4. Google review proof overlay near the hero photo
5. Proof row with experience, warranty, callback promise, and Google rating
6. Process band explaining the four-step flow
7. Supporting project proof using Woodville and Stevenson Church photos
8. Quote CTA band reinforcing the one-business-day callback

## Hero Direction

The selected hero image is:

`marketing-assets/project-photos/raw/Scottsboro New Construction/House.jpeg`

Reason:

- It supports the process-first direction.

The hero should pair that photo with copy that keeps homeowners included, so the page does not feel builder-only.

Recommended hero message:

Approved cities:

- Scottsboro
- Huntsville
- Gadsden
- Fort Payne

## Proof and Trust Treatment

The homepage should show trust signals early, preferably in a proof row directly below the hero.

Approved proof points:

- 30 years combined construction experience
- 2-year labor warranty
- 1 business day callback
- 5.0 Google review rating

The proof row should feel sturdy and factual, not decorative.

The Ashley J. Google review is the strongest homepage review:

> "They showed up when they said they would, performed super quality work and were professional all the way around!"
>
> - Ashley J., Google Review

Reason:

- It directly answers the reliability concern.
- It reinforces quality.
- It is short enough for homepage placement.

## Process Band

The homepage should include a strong process band with this structure:

> Measure carefully. Quote clearly. Install cleanly. Follow up.

Recommended steps:

- `01 Request`: Send your project details and photos through the quote form.
- `02 Call`: We call within one business day to understand the project.
- `03 Quote`: Your quote is based on actual details, not rushed guesses.
- `04 Install`: We install with quality standards and follow up afterward.

This band is central to the selected visual direction. It answers the two biggest objections:

- How much will it cost?
- Will you show up?

## Component Style

Approved component direction:

- Square or lightly rounded panels, not overly pill-shaped cards
- Clear rectangular CTA buttons
- City chips for service area visibility
- Proof/stat blocks with strong typography
- Photo-led project cards lower on the page
- Review overlay or review strip using real Google review text
- Full-width content bands rather than many floating cards

Buttons:

- Main CTA text: `Request a Quote`
- CTA color: red accent
- Secondary CTA examples: `See Our Work`, `View Services`

## Responsive Direction

The mobile version should preserve the same hierarchy:

- Logo and `Request a Quote` remain easy to access
- Hero copy appears before or near the hero photo
- Service-area cities remain visible without becoming cluttered
- Proof points stack cleanly
- Process steps stay readable
- Project proof photos remain large enough to inspect

The site should not hide the service-area message on mobile.

## Accessibility and Readability Notes

During implementation, the design should preserve:

- High contrast between text and background
- Large enough body text for mobile readers
- Clear focus states on buttons and form fields
- Alt text for meaningful photos
- Real text for reviews and proof points
- No text baked into images

The warm palette should not reduce readability. Cream and green should be tested for contrast before launch.

## Design Decisions Locked

- Visual lane: process-first regional trust
